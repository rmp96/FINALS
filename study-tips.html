Study Tips

Studying the right way can save you time and help you remember more. Try some of these tips to study smarter, not harder.

  • 1. Space out your study time: Study a little each day instead of all at once.
  • 2. Quiz yourself: Practice remembering things without looking at notes.
  • 3. Teach what you learned: If you can explain it to someone else, you know it well.
  • 4. Mix it up: Use different ways to study like writing, videos, and drawing.
  • 5. Take short breaks: Study for 25–30 minutes, then rest for 5 minutes.
  • 6. Avoid multitasking: Focus on one subject at a time for better results.
  • 7. Study in a quiet place: Fewer distractions help you stay focused.
  • 8. Review notes often: Don’t just take notes — go over them regularly.
  • 9. Make a study plan: Write down what and when you will study to stay on track.
  • 10. Use practice tests: Look for old quizzes or make your own to get ready for the real thing.
  • 11. Study with a friend: Ask questions, compare notes, and explain things to each other.
  • 12. Stay organized: Keep your materials and workspace neat so you can find what you need.
  • 13. Sleep well: Your brain needs rest to remember things you’ve studied.
  • 14. Eat healthy snacks: Fuel your brain with fruits, nuts, or water instead of junk food.
  • 15. Stay positive: Believe that you can learn it — confidence makes a big difference!
