Student Resources

Using good resources can make studying and learning much easier. The right websites can help explain confusing topics, prepare you for exams, and even offer practice problems and study guides. Below are some helpful tools that many students use.As well as mental health links as well.

  • Khan Academy – Free lessons on math, science, economics, and more. Great for step-by-step videos used to help students with hard concepts.
  • Chegg – Offers textbook solutions, tutoring help, and homework assistance. Some content is free and useful; others require a subscription.
  • Quizlet – Makes studying simple with flashcards, games, and quizzes. You can create your own sets or use ones from other students. One of the most used resources for students.
  • SparkNotes – Best for English and literature classes. It has book summaries, chapter breakdowns, and theme explanations.
  • Coursera – Offers free and paid courses from universities. Useful if you want to dive deeper into a subject or learn new skills.
  • NAMI for Students – The National Alliance on Mental Illness offers mental health education and support specifically for teens and college students.
  • The Jed Foundation – Focuses on protecting emotional health and preventing suicide among young adults. Offers tools and advice for managing stress and anxiety.
